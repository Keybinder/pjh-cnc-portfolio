Brief

The brief was originally to design and construct a stool showing inspiration from the Bauhaus design style using the CNC overhead router as the primary means of shaping the parts; however, any small furniture item was also allowed as a design, given that it was Bauhaus-inspired.

Limitations were as follows:

  • Material was 14.8-15 mm plywood.
  • Available cutting area was 1200 x 600 mm.
    • An edge border of about 50 mm was needed to maintain vacuum pressure for the CNC milling machine and had to be taken into account when laying out the design.
  • The design had to incorporate 'tab and slot' style fitted assembly -- no adhesives, screws, etc. were to be used without checking with a master.
  • CNC milling machine drill bit diameter was 9.8mm, so strategies such as bridges and 'dogboning' were made necessary in processing designs that made precision cuts.

Despite these limitations, the CNC milling machine was an extremely advantageous tool/technique, making the mass production of parts more quickly and precisely than hand tools and techniques. Designs made for prototyping on the laser cutter could be scaled up and reused for the milling machine and vice versa: a good example of the efficiency that CAD tools and techniques can provide.
